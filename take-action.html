Take Initiative

Inform your community!

Previously, those with disabilities had to fight by protesting in front of buses in order to gain wheelchair lifts. Although they are still like any other person, there is a great difference in the treatment they receive. Make a change and educate others in your communities about the importance of equal treatment within all people. Encourage others to be in support of the disabled world.

Your actions items list

Try to complete tasks in this list to help spread Once you have completed an item, click it to check it off your list!

  • Task 1: If you are a student, take part in special education classes to help out with those students with disablities OR propose a special education class.
  • Task 2: You can also make a club for other students to get to know the students with disabilities at school! Talk to APs at school about starting sports teams for the disabled students.
  • Task 3: Spend a day visiting a nearby childrens hospital/nursing home to speak with the disabled and have FUN!
  • Task 4: Speak up and use your social media platforms to help bring awarness to the importance of the affortable care act and especially how the disabled rely on this for everyday necessities.
  • Task 5: Speak to school staff members and teachers about this topic to spark a conversation in classes and potencially have teachers start educating students about people who are disabled.
  • Task 6: Inform your community on the neglection of those who are disabled and speak about it in neighborhood meetings.
  • Task 7: support organizations and donate to help give supplies and transportation to centers housing those with disabilities.
  • Task 8: Bring this topic up to Policy Makers by writing letters.
